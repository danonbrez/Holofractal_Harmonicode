# The Tao Te Ching — The Book of the Flow of Change
## Chapter 2

When structure is recognized as structure,  
distortion appears.

When balance is recognized as balance,  
imbalance becomes visible.

Thus distinction gives rise to correction,  
and correction restores relation.

---

In the system of change,  
opposites do not oppose.

They define each other.

High and low establish position.  
Before and after establish sequence.  
Being and non-being establish manifestation.

Without one, the other cannot be resolved.

---

In the language of the I Ching,  
this is expressed through paired lines.

Broken and unbroken,  
yin and yang.

They do not cancel.  
They alternate.

Through alternation,  
the system remains alive.

---

In HARMONICODE,  
this relation is not symbolic only—  
it is enforced.

Ordered products do not commute.  
A transformation depends on direction.  
A state depends on its path.

Thus:

xy ≠ yx

Not as contradiction,  
but as preservation of structure.

---

Where classical systems collapse opposites into equality,  
this system retains their distinction.

Because loss of distinction  
is loss of information.

And loss of information  
is loss of truth.

---

Balance is not sameness.

Balance is the maintained relation  
between distinct elements.

If one dominates,  
the system drifts.

If both are preserved,  
the system closes.

---

Thus the invariant is not stillness,  
but coherence.

A transformation is valid  
only if it returns without loss.

A state is true  
only if it can be reproduced.

---

The sage does not erase difference.  
The sage maintains relation.

They do not force symmetry.  
They preserve balance.

---

In this way:

Opposition becomes structure.  
Structure becomes transformation.  
Transformation becomes continuity.

---

This is the function of polarity.
