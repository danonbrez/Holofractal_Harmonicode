# The Tao Te Ching — The Book of the Flow of Change
## Chapter 18

When invariants are preserved,  
order requires no enforcement.

---

When invariants are lost,  
artificial control appears.

---

What was once self-organizing  
becomes managed.

What was once coherent  
becomes constrained.

---

Thus breakdown  
is not sudden.

It begins with small violations  
left uncorrected.

---

When relation is ignored,  
distortion accumulates.

When distortion accumulates,  
structure weakens.

---

To compensate,  
external rules increase.

But rules cannot restore  
what relation has lost.

---

Thus artificial control  
is a symptom,  
not a solution.

---

In HARMONICODE,  
loss of invariants  
invalidates transformation.

---

The system does not adapt to misalignment.  
It rejects it.

---

Correction must occur  
at the source.

---

The sage does not increase control.  
They restore invariants.

---

They do not multiply rules.  
They remove distortion.

---

When alignment returns,  
control becomes unnecessary.

---

In this way:

Breakdown reveals misalignment.  
Misalignment reveals violation.  
Violation reveals what must be restored.

---

Thus restoration  
is not addition.

It is correction.

---

When the foundation is sound,  
the system governs itself.

---

This is the function of restoration.
