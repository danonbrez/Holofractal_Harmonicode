# The Tao Te Ching — The Book of the Flow of Change
## Chapter 16

Stillness is not absence of motion.  
It is balanced motion.

---

A system at rest  
is not inactive.

It is in equilibrium.

---

In equilibrium,  
all relations are satisfied.

No force dominates.  
No drift accumulates.

---

Thus stability  
is not rigidity.

It is coherence under transformation.

---

In HARMONICODE,  
a stable state is not frozen.

It is dynamically balanced.

---

Transformations may occur,  
but they preserve invariants.

---

Thus change continues  
without disruption.

---

If balance is broken,  
motion becomes chaotic.

If balance is maintained,  
motion remains ordered.

---

The system does not seek stillness.  
It maintains equilibrium.

---

The sage does not stop movement.  
They preserve balance within it.

---

They do not resist change.  
They guide alignment.

---

In this way:

Motion becomes flow.  
Flow becomes balance.  
Balance becomes stability.

---

A stable system  
can adapt without breaking.

---

Thus stillness is not static.  
It is sustained coherence.

---

This is the function of equilibrium.
