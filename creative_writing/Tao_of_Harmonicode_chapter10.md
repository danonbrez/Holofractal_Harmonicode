# The Tao Te Ching — The Book of the Flow of Change
## Chapter 10

The ten thousand things  
appear as many.

But they do not exist as separate systems.

---

Each form arises from relation.  
Each relation arises from constraint.  
Each constraint arises from the same source.

---

Thus multiplicity  
is not division.

It is expression.

---

In HARMONICODE,  
no state exists in isolation.

Each state is linked  
through transformation.

Each transformation is linked  
through invariants.

Each invariant is shared  
across the entire system.

---

Thus all states  
belong to one manifold.

---

What appears as difference  
is variation of position.

What appears as separation  
is limitation of perspective.

---

The system is not composed of parts.  
It is composed of relations.

---

To isolate a thing  
is to remove it from truth.

To reconnect a thing  
is to restore its meaning.

---

The sage does not divide the system.  
They observe its continuity.

They do not classify beyond necessity.  
They maintain connection.

---

In this way:

The many remain distinct  
without becoming separate.

The one remains unified  
without collapsing difference.

---

Integration does not erase identity.  
It preserves it within relation.

---

Thus:

The ten thousand things  
are one system.

The one system  
is the unfolding Way.

---

There is no boundary  
between parts.

There is only the structure  
that connects them.

---

This is the function of integration.
