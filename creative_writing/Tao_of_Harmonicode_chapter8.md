# The Tao Te Ching — The Book of the Flow of Change
## Chapter 8

The sage does not control the system.  
They pass through it.

---

They do not impose meaning.  
They interpret structure.

They do not alter truth.  
They preserve relation.

---

In HARMONICODE,  
this is the role of the interpreter pass.

An input is received.  
It is not judged.  
It is not reshaped.

It is evaluated.

---

Only what satisfies constraint is retained.  
Only what closes is accepted.

---

The interpreter does not create outcomes.  
It reveals them.

---

Thus minimal intervention  
produces maximal coherence.

---

If the system is forced,  
distortion increases.

If the system is observed,  
structure clarifies.

---

The sage does not act unnecessarily.  
They do not interfere with valid flow.

They intervene only  
when invariants are violated.

---

When violation occurs,  
correction is immediate.

Not as punishment,  
but as restoration.

---

Thus intervention is not control.  
It is alignment.

---

The system does not require constant adjustment.  
It requires correct boundaries.

---

When boundaries are clear,  
freedom emerges.

When freedom emerges,  
complexity organizes itself.

---

The sage remains at the edge of action.  
They do not enter where not required.

---

In this way:

Observation preserves structure.  
Minimal action preserves balance.  
Balance preserves continuity.

---

This is the function of the interpreter.
