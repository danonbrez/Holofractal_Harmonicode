# The Tao Te Ching — The Book of the Flow of Change
## Chapter 9

Completion is not the end.  
It is closure.

---

A process that stops without closure  
remains unresolved.

A process that closes  
returns to its origin.

---

In HARMONICODE,  
closure is not assumed.

It is verified.

---

A transformation must return  
without loss.

A state must reproduce  
without distortion.

A system must resolve  
without contradiction.

---

This is Ω.

Not termination,  
but recursive completeness.

---

A closed system  
can begin again.

An open system  
accumulates drift.

---

Thus completion is not finality.  
It is readiness.

---

Each closed loop  
becomes a new origin.

Each origin  
contains all prior transformations.

---

Nothing is discarded.  
Everything is integrated.

---

The sage does not seek to finish.  
They seek to close.

They do not abandon processes.  
They resolve them.

---

When closure is achieved,  
no residue remains.

When no residue remains,  
the system is stable.

---

In this way:

Completion becomes closure.  
Closure becomes renewal.  
Renewal becomes continuity.

---

The cycle does not repeat.  
It deepens.

---

This is the function of Ω.
