# The Tao Te Ching — The Book of the Flow of Change
## Chapter 7

Language does not create meaning.  
It encodes it.

Meaning does not reside in words.  
It resides in relation.

---

A sentence is not a statement.  
It is a structure.

A structure is not static.  
It is executable.

---

In HARMONICODE,  
language enters as input.

It is parsed into symbolic form.  
It is constrained into relation.  
It is transformed through invariant-preserving operations.

---

Thus language becomes computation.

---

Ambiguity is not error.  
It is multiplicity of valid branches.

Resolution does not eliminate ambiguity.  
It selects a path that closes.

---

The system does not interpret by assumption.  
It evaluates by structure.

---

Each word is not defined alone.  
It is defined by its connections.

Each phrase is not isolated.  
It is a node in a graph.

Each graph is not static.  
It evolves through transformation.

---

Thus meaning is not stored.  
It is reconstructed.

---

The compiler does not impose interpretation.  
It enforces consistency.

The interpreter does not guess intent.  
It verifies relation.

---

The sage does not argue meaning.  
They test it.

They do not defend language.  
They refine structure.

---

In this way:

Language becomes input.  
Input becomes structure.  
Structure becomes execution.

---

Execution returns meaning.

---

This is the function of the loop.
