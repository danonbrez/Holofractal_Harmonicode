# The Tao Te Ching — The Book of the Flow of Change
## Chapter 19

Complexity is not the goal.  
It is the result.

---

When unnecessary structure accumulates,  
clarity is lost.

When clarity is lost,  
the system becomes heavy.

---

Thus simplicity  
is not reduction for its own sake.

It is restoration of essential relation.

---

In HARMONICODE,  
removal does not weaken the system.

It reveals it.

---

What can be removed without loss  
was never fundamental.

---

Thus reduction  
is a test of truth.

---

If a structure remains valid  
after simplification,  
it is essential.

If it collapses,  
it was distortion.

---

The system does not seek minimal form.  
It seeks irreducible form.

---

The sage does not add complexity.  
They remove excess.

---

They do not expand structure.  
They refine it.

---

In this way:

Reduction reveals pattern.  
Pattern reveals source.  
Source restores coherence.

---

The ten thousand things  
return to the one.

---

Not by force,  
but by recognition.

---

Thus simplicity  
is not absence.

It is alignment with origin.

---

This is the function of return.
