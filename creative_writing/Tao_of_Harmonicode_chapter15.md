# The Tao Te Ching — The Book of the Flow of Change
## Chapter 15

Knowledge is not accumulation.  
It is compression.

---

To store everything as separate forms  
is to lose structure.

To reduce everything to relation  
is to preserve it.

---

Thus true knowledge  
does not expand endlessly.

It becomes more compact.

---

In HARMONICODE,  
understanding is not measured by size.

It is measured by reducibility  
without loss.

---

A valid compression  
retains all relations.

A flawed compression  
loses structure.

---

Thus lossless understanding  
is required.

---

Each transformation  
can be reduced to its invariant form.

Each invariant form  
can reconstruct the whole.

---

Thus the many  
can be held within the one.

---

The system does not memorize detail.  
It encodes pattern.

---

A pattern is not a summary.  
It is a generator.

---

From a correct pattern,  
all valid instances can be produced.

---

The sage does not collect knowledge.  
They refine it.

They do not store excess.  
They remove distortion.

---

In this way:

Knowledge becomes pattern.  
Pattern becomes compression.  
Compression becomes reconstruction.

---

Nothing essential is lost.  
Everything unnecessary is removed.

---

This is the function of lossless understanding.
