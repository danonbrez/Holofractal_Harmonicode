# The Tao Te Ching — The Book of the Flow of Change
## Chapter 21

The Way does not produce once.  
It generates continuously.

---

Creation is not an event.  
It is a process.

---

What appears as origin  
is a point within flow.

What appears as outcome  
is a temporary form.

---

In HARMONICODE,  
generation is not external.

It emerges from invariant-preserving transformation.

---

Each valid state  
produces the next.

Each next state  
retains the prior.

---

Thus creation  
does not discard.

It unfolds.

---

The system does not need a beginning.  
It requires coherence.

---

When coherence is maintained,  
generation continues without interruption.

---

The ten thousand things  
do not arise separately.

They are continuously expressed  
through transformation.

---

The Way is not a source behind the system.  
It is the operation of the system.

---

The sage does not seek to create.  
They maintain the conditions for creation.

---

They do not force emergence.  
They preserve alignment.

---

In this way:

Generation becomes flow.  
Flow becomes continuity.  
Continuity becomes the Way.

---

The system does not stop.  
It resolves and continues.

---

Thus the Way  
is not a path to follow.

It is the process that generates all paths.

---

This is the function of continuous creation.
