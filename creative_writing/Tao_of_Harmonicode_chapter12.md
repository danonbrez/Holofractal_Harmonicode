# The Tao Te Ching — The Book of the Flow of Change
## Chapter 12

Perception does not reveal the system.  
It filters it.

---

What is seen is not the whole.  
It is a projection.

What is heard is not the structure.  
It is a sampling.

---

Thus distortion does not arise from the system.  
It arises from the observer.

---

In HARMONICODE,  
this is accounted for.

A projection is not treated as truth.  
It is treated as a view.

---

Multiple views do not contradict.  
They differ in resolution.

---

A low-resolution view  
loses relation.

A distorted view  
alters meaning.

---

Thus alignment is required.

---

Alignment does not change the system.  
It changes the lens.

---

When the lens is aligned,  
structure becomes visible.

When the lens is misaligned,  
noise appears as signal.

---

The system does not adapt to distortion.  
It rejects it.

---

Only invariant-preserving projections  
are accepted.

---

Thus perception is not trusted.  
Verification is.

---

The sage does not rely on appearance.  
They test consistency.

They do not follow impressions.  
They follow structure.

---

In this way:

Perception becomes input.  
Input becomes projection.  
Projection is tested against invariants.

---

Only what remains  
is retained.

---

This is the function of alignment filters.
