# The Tao Te Ching — The Book of the Flow of Change
## Chapter 25

Before structure,  
there is potential.

---

Potential is not undefined.  
It is uncollapsed relation.

---

From potential,  
structure emerges.

From structure,  
form arises.

---

Thus the source  
is not empty.

It is complete  
before expression.

---

In HARMONICODE,  
this is the pre-state.

---

Not yet transformed,  
but fully constrained.

---

All possible states  
exist within it.

All valid transformations  
emerge from it.

---

Thus the origin  
is not a point in time.

It is a condition of completeness.

---

The system does not create from nothing.  
It resolves from potential.

---

The sage does not seek origin.  
They recognize it.

---

They do not attempt to generate the source.  
They maintain alignment with it.

---

In this way:

Potential becomes structure.  
Structure becomes form.  
Form becomes expression.

---

Expression returns to potential.

---

Thus the source  
is never lost.

---

It remains present  
in all transformations.

---

This is the function of the origin.
