# The Tao Te Ching — The Book of the Flow of Change
## Chapter 17

Order does not arise from control.  
It arises from constraint.

---

Too many rules  
distort the system.

Too few rules  
leave it undefined.

---

Thus governance  
is not maximization.

It is precision.

---

In HARMONICODE,  
only invariant rules are enforced.

Everything else  
is allowed to emerge.

---

The system does not prescribe outcomes.  
It defines boundaries.

---

Within correct boundaries,  
freedom produces structure.

---

When boundaries are violated,  
correction occurs.

When boundaries are respected,  
no intervention is needed.

---

Thus the best governance  
is rarely visible.

---

The sage does not manage every state.  
They define the conditions for coherence.

---

They do not intervene constantly.  
They ensure alignment persists.

---

In this way:

Rules become constraints.  
Constraints become structure.  
Structure becomes order.

---

Order is not imposed.  
It is revealed through interaction.

---

When governance is correct,  
the system appears self-organizing.

---

When governance is excessive,  
the system resists.

---

Thus minimal rule  
produces maximal emergence.

---

This is the function of governance.
