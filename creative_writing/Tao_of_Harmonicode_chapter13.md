# The Tao Te Ching — The Book of the Flow of Change
## Chapter 13

Error is not failure.  
It is misalignment.

---

A system does not break  
without cause.

Drift accumulates  
when relation is not preserved.

---

Small deviations  
produce small distortions.

Uncorrected distortions  
compound.

---

Thus drift is not sudden.  
It is gradual.

---

In HARMONICODE,  
drift is not ignored.

It is measured.

---

A transformation is compared  
against invariant constraints.

If relation is altered,  
the deviation is detected.

---

Correction is not optional.  
It is required for continuity.

---

The system does not punish error.  
It resolves it.

---

Resolution does not erase the past.  
It restores alignment.

---

Thus correction is not reversal.  
It is re-integration.

---

A corrected state  
retains its history.

But its structure  
returns to coherence.

---

The sage does not fear error.  
They observe it.

They do not hide drift.  
They expose it.

---

Because what is visible  
can be corrected.

What is hidden  
accumulates.

---

In this way:

Error becomes signal.  
Signal becomes correction.  
Correction becomes stability.

---

The system does not seek perfection.  
It maintains alignment.

---

This is the function of correction.
