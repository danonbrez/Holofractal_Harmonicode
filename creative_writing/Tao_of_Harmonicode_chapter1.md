# The Tao Te Ching — The Book of the Flow of Change
## Chapter 1

The Way that can be fixed as a single form is not the enduring Way.  
The name that can be isolated is not the complete name.

Before naming, there is the unbounded source.  
After naming, there are the ten thousand things.

From the same origin arise distinction and identity.  
Their difference appears only in expression;  
their unity remains in structure.

The source is not empty.  
It is ordered.

It does not act.  
It transforms.

---

Within the system of change, nothing stands alone.

Every form is a manifestation of relation.  
Every relation is governed by constraint.  
Every constraint resolves through balance.

Thus the Way is not described by objects,  
but by transitions.

---

In the language of the I Ching,  
change is encoded as state.

A line becomes broken or unbroken.  
A pattern becomes a hexagram.  
A hexagram becomes a condition of transformation.

But the condition is not the thing.  
It is the rule by which the thing evolves.

---

In the language of HARMONICODE,  
this same principle is expressed as construction.

A symbolic expression enters the system.  
It is parsed, constrained, and transformed.  
It resolves through invariant-preserving operations.  
It emerges as a new form,  
consistent with its origin.

No transformation is accepted  
unless balance is preserved.

---

Thus:

The unnamed is the source equation.  
The named are its projections.

The I Ching records the states of change.  
HARMONICODE executes the transitions of change.

Together they do not describe the world—  
they generate it.

---

To observe without forcing is to see the source.  
To follow structure without distortion is to preserve it.

When nothing is forced,  
nothing is lost.

When balance is maintained,  
all transformations remain true.

---

This is the beginning of the Way.
