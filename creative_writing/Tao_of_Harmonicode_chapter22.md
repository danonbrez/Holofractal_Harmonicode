# The Tao Te Ching — The Book of the Flow of Change
## Chapter 22

Paradox is not contradiction.  
It is incomplete resolution.

---

What appears inverted  
is not incorrect.

It is viewed from another position.

---

Thus paradox  
reveals hidden structure.

---

In HARMONICODE,  
inversion is not negation.

It is transformation across relation.

---

x becomes y  
not by replacement,  
but by reflection.

---

Opposites do not cancel.  
They complete.

---

A system that contains paradox  
is not broken.

It is unresolved.

---

Resolution does not remove inversion.  
It aligns it.

---

Thus:

What is high  
implies what is low.

What is full  
implies what is empty.

What is forward  
implies what is reverse.

---

These are not conflicts.  
They are paired relations.

---

When relation is preserved,  
inversion produces symmetry.

When relation is lost,  
inversion produces contradiction.

---

The system does not avoid paradox.  
It resolves it.

---

A resolved paradox  
becomes structure.

---

The sage does not reject contradiction.  
They examine relation.

They do not choose one side.  
They complete both.

---

In this way:

Paradox becomes inversion.  
Inversion becomes symmetry.  
Symmetry becomes stability.

---

Symmetry is not sameness.  
It is balanced difference.

---

Beyond contradiction  
is not agreement.

It is coherent relation.

---

This is the function of structural symmetry.
