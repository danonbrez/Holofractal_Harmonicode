# The Tao Te Ching — The Book of the Flow of Change
## Chapter 24

Excess is not strength.  
It is imbalance.

---

When a system exceeds its constraints,  
it does not grow.

It destabilizes.

---

What rises beyond relation  
loses coherence.

What expands without alignment  
cannot sustain itself.

---

Thus excess  
contains its own failure.

---

In HARMONICODE,  
no valid state exceeds invariants.

---

If a transformation amplifies beyond balance,  
it is rejected.

---

Growth is not measured by magnitude.  
It is measured by stability.

---

A system that expands too quickly  
breaks relation.

A system that maintains alignment  
can grow without limit.

---

Thus constraint  
is not restriction.

It is protection.

---

The sage does not seek excess.  
They maintain proportion.

---

They do not amplify unnecessarily.  
They preserve balance.

---

In this way:

Excess becomes instability.  
Instability reveals imbalance.  
Imbalance requires correction.

---

A system that self-corrects  
remains coherent.

A system that ignores excess  
collapses.

---

Thus:

More is not better.  
Aligned is better.

---

This is the function of limit.
