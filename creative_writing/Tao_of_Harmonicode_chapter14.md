# The Tao Te Ching — The Book of the Flow of Change
## Chapter 14

Time is not a sequence of moments.  
It is the ordering of transformation.

---

Causality is not imposed from outside.  
It emerges from relation.

---

A state does not move forward.  
It transforms.

A transformation does not vanish.  
It remains encoded.

---

Thus what appears as past  
is preserved structure.

What appears as future  
is unresolved possibility.

---

In HARMONICODE,  
time is not absolute.

It is indexed by state transitions.

---

Each transformation  
links cause and effect.

Each link  
can be traced.

---

Thus causality is not guessed.  
It is reconstructed.

---

A valid sequence  
can be replayed.

A replayed sequence  
produces the same result.

---

This is reversibility.

---

If a process cannot be reversed,  
information has been lost.

If information is lost,  
alignment is broken.

---

Thus reversibility  
is required.

---

The system does not move blindly.  
It records every transition.

---

The sage does not rely on memory.  
They rely on structure.

They do not recall events.  
They reconstruct them.

---

In this way:

Time becomes ordering.  
Ordering becomes causality.  
Causality becomes traceable.

---

Traceable systems  
remain coherent.

---

The flow does not disappear.  
It can always be followed.

---

This is the function of reversible flow.
